# Importance Scoring

Use this scoring framework to rank AI news for a primary-market investor.

## Scoring Dimensions

Score each item from 1 to 5 on the following dimensions:

- Strategic impact: does it change the market structure or stack economics?
- Startup relevance: does it create or destroy room for startups?
- Capital relevance: does it affect funding, valuation, exits, or capital flows?
- China relevance: does it matter specifically for China market access, substitution, or regulation?
- Durability: is it likely to matter beyond the current news cycle?

## Rating Guide

### High Importance

Usually scores high on at least three dimensions and affects one of:

- foundation model capability or access
- chip, cloud, or inference economics
- policy and regulatory permission structures
- platform distribution power
- large financing or M&A with strategic consequences

### Medium Importance

Worth monitoring because it may develop into a stronger signal, such as:

- notable enterprise adoption
- important but not decisive model releases
- management changes or partnerships with clear follow-up risk
- early product traction with strategic relevance

### Low Importance

Do not lead with these unless the user explicitly asks:

- incremental feature launches
- generic ecosystem partnerships
- thinly sourced rumors
- commentary without new factual development
- articles that are interesting but not investable

## Output Rule

When ranking items, include a one-line explanation for why the item sits in High,
Medium, or Low and mention the strongest scoring dimensions.
