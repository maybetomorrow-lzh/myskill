# Investor Lens

Use this file to tune the digest toward the user's actual investment style.

## Default Focus

- China and cross-border AI developments
- primary-market implications instead of public-market price action
- startup formation, funding momentum, and moat shifts
- model, application, infrastructure, chip, and policy developments
- technical trend changes before broad market consensus forms

## Preferred Media

Use these Chinese media outlets actively for discovery and context:

- Jiqizhixin
- QbitAI
- Xinzhiyuan
- Pedaily
- Huxiu AI coverage
- 36Kr AI coverage

Treat them as important discovery channels, then verify key facts with primary
sources when possible.

## Rank Higher By Default

- changes in compute supply or inference economics
- policy shifts affecting model deployment or data access
- major model releases that open or close startup opportunities
- large enterprise adoption signals tied to real budget movement
- financing, M&A, or shutdowns that reset category expectations
- technical iterations that materially improve capability, cost, latency, reliability, or deployment feasibility
- company execution that shows category leadership in a strategically important niche

## Technical Deep-Dive Rules

When an important technical development appears, include:

- what technically changed
- whether the change is incremental or step-function
- which layer it affects: model, data, tooling, chip, infra, or application
- whether it improves cost, performance, deployment speed, or defensibility
- what kinds of startups or incumbents benefit or get pressured
- whether the effect is near-term or likely to compound

## Company Tracking Bias

Give extra attention to AI companies that show unusually strong performance in a
specific domain. Summarize:

- what they are doing well
- what evidence supports that judgment
- whether the advantage looks product, distribution, data, talent, or compute driven
- whether the signal suggests a broader market trend

## Rank Lower By Default

- superficial feature launches
- weakly sourced rumors
- generic conference commentary
- partnership announcements without distribution or revenue implications
- trend pieces with no new fact pattern
